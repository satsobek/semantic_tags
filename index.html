The Back Story

I was originaly born in Baltimore Maryland, but grew up in the Seattle area. I was a huge nerd growing up- obsessed with History and Science. I spent countless summers Cival War Re-eniacting with my parents and volunteering in the Appplied Physics Lab at the University of Washington. For most of my childhood I wanted to become a neurosurgeon and attend Johns Hopkins!

College changed my path, as I decided to study Forensic Anthropology. While I passionately enjoyed studying human remains (I know, kinda creepy), I found the criminilistic components of my studies boring. I was taking some Archaeology and Geology classes as electives and decided to jump ship! So I became an Archaeology major with an emphasis on preshitoric artificat analysis and conflict studies. I still got to study ancient remains and solve mysteries without the boring crimnal and law studies.

When I graduated College I took a year off to be an AmeriCorp Volunteer where I taught children with Autism for one year in Walla Walla. I also volunteered at the Fort Walla Walla Museum, and when my AmeriCorp service was over I started working for the museum as an Arhcaeologist. I worked as a Field Archaeologist and Lab Manager for 3 years before we lost funding and I moved back to Seattle.

These days I am working for the Office of Animal Welfare at the UW doing aboslutely nothing related to Archaeology. I was originally hired as an Administrative Assistant but was moved into a more technical position after I implemented SharePoint in our Office. I taught myself how to use SharePoint and fell in love with it (I know, I'm weird!!). SharePoint is actually my gateway into Computer Science and Web Development. I also serve as a technical consultant in our eIACUC solution, HoverBoard.
